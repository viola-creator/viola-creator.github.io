About the home

A machiya's rebirth

Inspired by the daily rhythm of Kyoto, Maana Kamo slows down modern life along the historical river, tuning into the vibrant colors, sounds, and bustle of the city. A designer's favourite, this home blurs the line between art and architecture, turning a traditional machiya into a sleek and seamless space.

Maana Kamo was one of the first projects to push the boundaries of machiya design. The original house was found in a neglected condition — covered in vinyl wallcoverings, tile ceilings, and broken floorboards. Pulling back the layers of synthetic materials revealed the imperfect, time-worn beauty of its foundations.

An urushi-lacquered kitchen island and a dramatically oversized onsen-style bathtub sit at the heart of the home, made for cooking together, soaking for too long, and staying in. Were it not for shadows, there would be no beauty.

Architecture · 建築

Beauty in shadows

Sleek and expansive, Maana Kamo redefines intentional living within the original 100-year-old frame. Dramatically lit spaces are designed to reveal the beauty in shadows — a quiet nod to Jun'ichirō Tanizaki's In Praise of Shadows.

The design reinforces Maana's focus on authentic craft and calm through structural preservation rather than surface repairs. A visually understated yet bold space, an invitation to pause.

Interior · 室内

Designed around the bathtub

An urushi-lacquered kitchen island and a dramatically oversized onsen-style bathtub sit at the heart of the home, made for cooking together, soaking for too long, and staying in.

Interior elements are pulled back and minimum, with antiques and artisanal crafts holding presence in the home, while Kyoto continues to echo and evolve outside.
